2. Treatment History & Resistance Mechanisms

Key Therapies Attempted:

  • Cetuximab + Bevacizumab (CEA ↓129.5 → 63.6): Partial response but short-lived.
  • FOLFOXIRI + Docetaxel (CEA ↑252.4): Chemoresistance (mucinous histology).
  • Nivolumab/Ipilimumab → Pembrolizumab (CEA ↓36.1 → 7.6): Initial response but eventual progression.

Identified Resistance Drivers:

  1. WNT/β-catenin Activation (APC/RNF43 mutations) → Immune exclusion (PD-L1=0, TPS=0).
  2. TP53 R273C → Chemoresistance and genomic instability.
  3. MEK1 K57T (MAP2K1) → Potential RAS/MAPK pathway bypass.

4. Prognosis & Monitoring

Short-Term (3–6 months):

  • High risk of visceral metastasis (liver/lung) due to TP53/APC-driven genomic instability.
  • CEA trend critical; if ↑>100, consider palliative RT for symptomatic nodes.

Long-Term:

  • Explore clinical trials for MEK inhibitors + immunotherapy or p53-targeted therapies.
  • Repeat liquid biopsy if ECOG permits (track emerging KRAS/NRAS clones).
Conclusion

Despite MSI-H, hyperprogression on immunotherapy and mucinous transformation necessitate a shift to targeted strategies (MEK inhibition, p53 reactivation) or clinical trials. WNT pathway inhibition (e.g., PORCN inhibitors) could be explored off-label.
